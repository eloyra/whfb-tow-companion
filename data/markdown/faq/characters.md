# Characters - Frequently Asked Questions

[View complete list](../faq.md) of all frequently asked questions.

Currently displaying all results.

### [Can my Battle Standard Bearer have a higher Leadership than my General?](#can-my-battle-standard-bearer-have-a-higher-leadership-than-my-general)

No. As stated on page 203, the General is the character with the highest Leadership. If this means not upgrading the character with the highest Leadership in your army to be your Battle Standard Bearer so that they can be your General, so be it.

Source: Official Warhammer: The Old World FAQ & Errata - Version 1.5.2

### [Can my army include a character that has a higher Leadership than my General if that character has a special rule (Loner, for example) that prevents them from being the General?](#can-my-army-include-a-character-that-has-a-higher-leadership-than-my-general)

Yes.

Source: Official Warhammer: The Old World FAQ & Errata - Version 1.5.2

### [If my army includes a Mercenary character, can I make them my General?](#if-my-army-includes-a-mercenary-character-can-i-make-them-my-general)

No. An army’s General must be one of the characters listed in the 'characters' section of the army composition list you are using.

Source: Official Warhammer: The Old World FAQ & Errata - Version 1.5.2

### [If a unit that is subject to any Leadership modifiers uses the General's Inspiring Presence rule (or any other rule that allows a unit to use the Leadership of a nearby character), does the unit ignore those modifiers?](#if-a-unit-that-is-subject-to-any-leadership-modifiers-uses-the-generals)

No, the modifier will still apply. For example, if a unit loses a round of combat to a Terror causing enemy, the -1 modifier to Leadership caused by Terror applies to whichever Leadership characteristic the unit makes its Break test against, be that its own or that of a nearby character.

Source: Official Warhammer: The Old World FAQ & Errata - Version 1.5.2

### [If my Battle Standard Bearer refuses a challenge and retires from combat, can other units still benefit from their "Hold Your Ground" special rule?](#if-my-battle-standard-bearer-refuses-a-challenge-and-retires-from-combat-can)

Not whilst that character's unit is still engaged with the model that issued the challenge. Battle standards are somewhat less inspirational whilst being used as a blanket to hide beneath.

Source: Official Warhammer: The Old World FAQ & Errata - Version 1.5.2

### [When a character takes a chariot as a mount, do they replace one of the crew?](#when-a-character-takes-a-chariot-as-a-mount-do-they-replace-one-of-the-crew)

No. The points you pay for a chariot include its crew, whether that chariot is included in your army on its own or as a mount for a character. However, in reality it might prove difficult to fit a character and the full complement of crew into the chariot model itself. If so, it is perfectly acceptable to remove one or more of the crew models to make space for the character. In such cases, we can think of the missing crew model's profile(s) as a way of representing that the beasts pulling a character's chariot are bound to be bigger, stronger and better trained than normal.

Source: Official Warhammer: The Old World FAQ & Errata - Version 1.5.2

### [A character mounted on a ridden monster or a chariot can choose to use their own or their mount's armour value, whichever is better. If the character wears magic armour but I choose to use the mount's armour value, can I still claim other benefits conferred by the magic armour?](#a-character-mounted-on-a-ridden-monster-or-a-chariot-can-choose-to-use-their)

No. You must use a magic item fully or not at all.

Source: Official Warhammer: The Old World FAQ & Errata - Version 1.5.2

### [If I have a character with an armour value of 6+ mounted on a chariot with an armour value of 4+, for example, am I obliged to use the chariot's armour value?](#if-i-have-a-character-with-an-armour-value-of-6-mounted-on-a-chariot-with-an)

No, you choose whichever armour value is better. However, allowing for magical armour and other special rules, 'better' is somewhat subjective. Obviously an armour value of 4+ is better than one of 6+ but there is often more to deciding which armour to use than just the armour value it offers, many types of magic armour confer additional bonuses that make them better. Basically, when the rules say 'choose', they are giving you a choice.

Source: Official Warhammer: The Old World FAQ & Errata - Version 1.5.2

### [If a character mounted on a ridden monster or chariot carries a shield, does that improve the mount's armour value?](#if-a-character-mounted-on-a-ridden-monster-or-chariot-carries-a-shield-does)

No, a shield is carried by the character, not the mount.

Source: Official Warhammer: The Old World FAQ & Errata - Version 1.5.2

### [Does the presence in a unit of a character whose base takes up the space of two or more models in two or more ranks reduce that unit’s rank bonus?](#does-the-presence-in-a-unit-of-a-character-whose-base-takes-up-the-space-of)

No.

Source: Official Warhammer: The Old World FAQ & Errata - Version 1.5.2

### [A Clumsy unit cannot be joined by a character that is not also Clumsy, but can a Clumsy character join a unit that is not Clumsy?](#a-clumsy-unit-cannot-be-joined-by-a-character-that-is-not-also-clumsy-but)

Source: Official Warhammer: The Old World FAQ & Errata - Version 1.5.2

### [Does a unit that is joined by a character of a different troop type gain the rules associated with the character’s troop type?](#does-a-unit-that-is-joined-by-a-character-of-a-different-troop-type-gain-the)

No.

Source: Official Warhammer: The Old World FAQ & Errata - Version 1.5.2

### [Can a character who is within the fighting rank of their unit but not in base contact with the enemy move through the ranks so that they are?](#can-a-character-who-is-within-the-fighting-rank-of-their-unit-but-not-in)

No. Characters can move through the ranks into the fighting rank, but cannot move within the fighting rank.

Source: Official Warhammer: The Old World FAQ & Errata - Version 1.5.2

### [Can a character be placed amongst a unit's command group?](#can-a-character-be-placed-amongst-a-units-command-group)

Yes.

Source: Official Warhammer: The Old World FAQ & Errata - Version 1.5.2

### [If there is no space for a character in the front rank of a unit with the Press of Battle rule, can they join the second rank?](#if-there-is-no-space-for-a-character-in-the-front-rank-of-a-unit-with-the)

No, characters must be placed in the front rank. If this is not possible, they retire to the rear.

Source: Official Warhammer: The Old World FAQ & Errata - Version 1.5.2

### [Can characters displace command group models into the second rank of a unit with the Press of Battle rule?](#can-characters-displace-command-group-models-into-the-second-rank-of-a-unit)

No, command group models must always be in the front rank of their unit.

Source: Official Warhammer: The Old World FAQ & Errata - Version 1.5.2

### [If a character is in the back rank of a unit due to there not being enough space for them in the front rank, do they confer any bonuses onto the unit?](#if-a-character-is-in-the-back-rank-of-a-unit-due-to-there-not-being-enough)

Unless they have a special rule stating otherwise, no.

Source: Official Warhammer: The Old World FAQ & Errata - Version 1.5.2

### [If a Warband character (as described on page 180) joins a Warband with a Rank Bonus of one or more, does the modifier to their Leadership characteristic increase their Command range?](#if-a-warband-character-as-described-on-page-180-joins-a-warband-with-a-rank-bonu)

Yes.

Source: Official Warhammer: The Old World FAQ & Errata - Version 1.5.2

### [My unit wishes to shoot at an enemy lone character. However, an enemy unit that is more than 3" away from that character presents a closer target. Can I shoot at the lone character?](#my-unit-wishes-to-shoot-at-an-enemy-lone-character-however-an-enemy-unit)

Yes. The rule that a lone character cannot be shot at unless they are the closest target only extends to units they are within 3" of. If a unit is more than 3" from the character, it does not protect them, regardless of how close it is to the enemy.

Source: Official Warhammer: The Old World FAQ & Errata - Version 1.5.2

### [Can a character that has joined a unit of models of a different troop type be targeted by enemy shooting? For example, if a character whose troop type is 'light chariot' joins a unit of 'regular infantry', can they be targeted?](#can-a-character-that-has-joined-a-unit-of-models-of-a-different-troop-type)

Yes. What's more, they don't get a "Look Out, Sir!" save either.

Source: Official Warhammer: The Old World FAQ & Errata - Version 1.5.2

### [If a character joins a unit that carries a magic standard, do its effects extend to the character?](#if-a-character-joins-a-unit-that-carries-a-magic-standard-do-its-effects)

Yes. When a character joins a unit they become part of that unit, so magic standards that affect the unit affect them as well.

Source: Official Warhammer: The Old World FAQ & Errata - Version 1.5.2

### [If a Warband character joins a unit that isn't a Warband, does that unit's Rank Bonus modify their Leadership characteristic?](#if-a-warband-character-joins-a-unit-that-isnt-a-warband-does-that-units-rank)

No.

Source: Official Warhammer: The Old World FAQ & Errata - Version 1.5.2

### [Cannon do not directly target enemy models; they target a point on the ground. How does this work with Targeting Lone characters? Can a cannon be fired in such a way as to hit a Lone character that would normally be protected from shooting by their proximity to a friendly unit?](#cannon-do-not-directly-target-enemy-models-they-target-a-point-on-the-ground-how)

The purpose of the Targeting Lone Characters rule is to protect characters from enemy shooting, even from shooting that does not follow the usual rules (though it does not protect them from templates). Therefore, and in the spirit of this rule, unless they are the closest target to the cannon, an enemy character that is within 3" of a friendly unit that contains five or more models of the same troop type cannot be struck by a cannonball, even if their base lies directly under the path of the bouncing cannonball. However, if a Lone character's base lies directly underneath the strike point of a cannonball, they are hit.

Source: Official Warhammer: The Old World FAQ & Errata - Version 1.5.2

### [Some magic weapons or special rules allow specific models within a unit to be targeted. Do such attacks ignore the "Look Out, Sir!" rule?](#some-magic-weapons-or-special-rules-allow-specific-models-within-a-unit-to)

No.

Source: Official Warhammer: The Old World FAQ & Errata - Version 1.5.2

### [If a named character is equipped with a mundane weapon such as a lance or cavalry spear and a magic weapon, can they choose to use the lance or cavalry spear, or must they use their magic weapon?](#if-a-named-character-is-equipped-with-a-mundane-weapon-such-as-a-lance-or-cavalr)

They must use their magic weapon. However, some named characters will carry a 'back-up' weapon which they can use should their magic weapon be destroyed.

Source: Official Warhammer: The Old World FAQ & Errata - Version 1.5.2
