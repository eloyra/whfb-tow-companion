### [Can a Steam Tank's steam cannon fire grapeshot?](#can-a-steam-tanks-steam-cannon-fire-grapeshot)

Yes. Grapeshot can be fired by any cannon (i.e., any weapon that fires using the 'Cannon Fire' special rule), as described on page 226 of the *Warhammer: The Old World* rulebook.

Source: Official Forces of Fantasy FAQ & Errata - Version 1.5.2

### [Can a Steam Tank's Engineer Commander and steam cannon both shoot during the same turn?](#can-a-steam-tanks-engineer-commander-and-steam-cannon-both-shoot-during-the)

Yes. The Engineer Commander doesn't fire the steam cannon, imagine there's a (very hot and bothered) crew hidden away inside the Steam Tank whose endeavours leave the Engineer Commander free to fire their own weapon.

Source: Official Forces of Fantasy FAQ & Errata - Version 1.5.2

### [Before a Steam Tank charges, can it use the Steam Power special rule to increase its Movement?](#before-a-steam-tank-charges-can-it-use-the-steam-power-special-rule-to)

No. The Steam Power special rule can be used by a Steam Tanks, a model that cannot march, instead of marching. In other words, in instances where most models or units would increase their movement by marching, a Steam Tank can instead make use of the Steam Power special rule. Charging is not such an instance.

Source: Official Forces of Fantasy FAQ & Errata - Version 1.5.2

### [Are unsaved wounds caused by the mount of a model bearing the Laurels of Victory worth 2 combat result points?](#are-unsaved-wounds-caused-by-the-mount-of-a-model-bearing-the-laurels-of)

No, only unsaved wounds caused by the bearer (the rider) count.

Source: Official Forces of Fantasy FAQ & Errata - Version 1.5.2

### [If a model bearing the Laurels of Victory Stands & Shoots as a charge reaction, causing one or more unsaved wounds, are those unsaved wounds each worth 2 combat result points?](#if-a-model-bearing-the-laurels-of-victory-stands-and-shoots-as-a-charge)

No.

Source: Official Forces of Fantasy FAQ & Errata - Version 1.5.2

### [Does the Griffon Standard allow a unit to claim a higher Rank Bonus than normal for its troop type?](#does-the-griffon-standard-allow-a-unit-to-claim-a-higher-rank-bonus-than)

Yes. As stated in the item's rules, a unit carrying the Griffon Standard can claim a Rank Bonus of +2 for each extra rank behind the first, rather than the usual +1, meaning a unit with two extra ranks could claim a Rank Bonus of +4, rather than the usual +2.

Source: Official Forces of Fantasy FAQ & Errata - Version 1.5.2
