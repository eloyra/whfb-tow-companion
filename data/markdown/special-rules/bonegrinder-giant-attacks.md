# Bonegrinder Giant Attacks

*Bonegrinder Giants do not attack in the same way as other creatures. They are far too large and fractious to take orders and much too scatter-brained to have any sort of coherent plan.*

Instead of attacking normally during the Combat phase, a Bonegrinder Giant must make a 'Bonegrinder Giant Attack'. To do so, nominate an enemy unit that the Bonegrinder Giant is engaged in combat with to be the target of the attack and roll on the Bonegrinder Giant Attacks table below. The Troop Type of the target unit determines whether it is a 'little thing', a 'big thing' or a 'bigger thing':

- **Little Things:** Units whose Troop Type is [regular infantry](../troop-types-in-detail/regular-infantry.md), [heavy infantry](../troop-types-in-detail/heavy-infantry.md), [swarms](../troop-types-in-detail/swarms.md), [light cavalry](../troop-types-in-detail/light-cavalry.md), [heavy cavalry](../troop-types-in-detail/heavy-cavalry.md) or [war beasts](../troop-types-in-detail/war-beasts.md).
- **Big Things:** Units whose Troop Type is [monstrous infantry](../troop-types-in-detail/monstrous-infantry.md), [monstrous cavalry](../troop-types-in-detail/monstrous-cavalry.md), [light chariot](../troop-types-in-detail/light-chariots.md) or [war machine](../troop-types-in-detail/war-machines.md).
- **Bigger Things:** Units whose Troop Type is [heavy chariot](../troop-types-in-detail/heavy-chariots.md), [monstrous creature](../troop-types-in-detail/monstrous-creatures.md) or [behemoth](../troop-types-in-detail/behemoths.md).
